About Kinnaur Valley

Located at a distance of about 235 kilometers from Shimla, Kinnaur Valley is a mystical land where ancient traditions meet stunning landscapes. The valley is divided into two parts - Lower Kinnaur and Upper Kinnaur, each offering unique experiences and breathtaking views.

Sangla Valley

A picturesque valley nestled in the Himalayas, known for its apple orchards, traditional wooden houses, and breathtaking views of the Kinner Kailash range.

  • Lower Kinnaur
  • Altitude: 2,800m
  • Best Time: April-October

Chitkul

The last inhabited village near the Indo-Tibet border, offering stunning landscapes, traditional Kinnauri architecture, and a glimpse into local culture.

  • Sangla Valley
  • Altitude: 3,450m
  • Best Time: May-September

Kalpa

An ancient village perched on a hill, offering panoramic views of the Kinner Kailash range and a rich cultural heritage with its temples and monasteries.

  • Lower Kinnaur
  • Altitude: 2,960m
  • Best Time: March-October
